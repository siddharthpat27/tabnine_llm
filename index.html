Chicken Chow Mein

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 (8-ounce) package dry egg noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (8-ounce) can sliced water chestnuts, drained
  • 1 (16-ounce) package frozen stir-fry vegetables
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the noodles according to the package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large wok or skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken and cook until no longer pink, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the water chestnuts, stir-fry vegetables, chicken broth, soy sauce, cornstarch, sesame oil, ginger, red pepper flakes, and cooked noodles. Stir to combine and cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.
  5. Serve hot over cooked rice.

Sweet and Sour Pork

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless pork loin, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (15-ounce) can pineapple chunks, drained
  • 1 (11-ounce) can mandarin oranges, drained
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, place the flour, eggs, and panko bread crumbs. Stir to combine.
  2. Add the pork pieces to the bowl and toss to coat.
  3. In a large wok or skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the pork pieces and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the pork from the wok and set aside.
  5. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the wok and heat over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  6. Add the pineapple, oranges, ketchup, vinegar, brown sugar, cornstarch, and cooked pork. Stir to combine and cook until the sauce thickens, about 5 minutes.
  7. Serve hot over cooked rice.
